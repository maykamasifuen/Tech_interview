**Resources**

A curated list of engineers, writers, and thinkers worth following. Their writing defines how senior engineers think — about systems, tradeoffs, organizations, and careers.

---

**Engineering Blogs & Newsletters**

-   [The Pragmatic Engineer - Gergely Orosz](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/) — deep industry insight, engineering culture, career, compensation
-   [Martin Fowler's Blog](https://martinfowler.com/) — architecture, refactoring, software design patterns; foundational reading
-   [Paul Graham's Essays](http://paulgraham.com/articles.html) — startups and how to think clearly about building things
-   [Joel Spolsky - Joel on Software](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/) — classic software engineering essays (hiring, product, engineering culture)
-   [Dan Luu's Blog](https://danluu.com/) — rigorous data-driven essays on engineering practices and performance
-   [Simon Willison's Blog](https://simonwillison.net/) — AI, LLMs, open source; the most thorough AI engineering commentary
-   [Will Larson - Irrational Exuberance](https://lethain.com/) — engineering management, staff engineering, systems
-   [Charity Majors](https://charity.wtf/) — observability, engineering leadership, honeycomb.io founder
-   [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/) — signal-heavy aggregator; follow the comment threads, not just headlines

**Must-Read Articles**

-   [Being Glue - Tanya Reilly](https://noidea.dog/glue) — on invisible work and why it matters for senior ICs

-   [Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule - Paul Graham](http://paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html) — why interruptions destroy deep work
-   [On Being a Senior Engineer - Kitchen Soap](https://www.kitchensoap.com/2012/10/25/on-being-a-senior-engineer/) — the most referenced definition of senior
